Buddhism

Warding Off Supernaturals, Their Practices And Their Beliefs

Buddhism ward off ghosts by chanting and sprinking holy water. The Buddhist believes that there is a need to perform the correct rites to ensure that the departed ancestor does not become a wandering ghost. If the correct rites is not performed, it could affect the fate of the living relatives. They also have practices such as celebrating the "Hungry Ghost Festival". The "Hungry Ghost Festival" takes place on the fifteenth day of the seventh month in the lunar calendar is called "Ghost Day" and the seventh month itself is considered as the "Ghost Month", in which they believe that the spirits and ghosts including those of the deceased ancestors, come out from the lower realm. During the "Hungry Ghost Festival" the Buddhist will prepare food offerings and burning joss paper, a papier-mache form of material items such as clothes, gold and other fine goods for the visiting spirits of the ancestors. Elaborate meals would also be served for each of the deceased in the family treating the deceased as if they are still living.
